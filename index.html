The problem

What does it mean for your company to be understood?

A great chief of staff, six months in, knows who actually decides, which Slack channel is real and which is performative, and what changed last week. That's understanding — and it's what an AI agent with access to your Drive, Slack, and CRM doesn't have.

Access means an agent can reach your data. Understanding means it knows what that data means.

Five ways access alone fails
01

Sources contradict.

Slack says Friday. Linear says Wednesday. Your agent picks whichever it finds first.

02

Identities fragment.

Lisa Chen shows up as five strings across five tools. Retrieval treats them as strangers.

03

Facts decay.

Your January doc is three pivots behind. Nothing tells you that.

04

Sources aren't equal.

A signed contract outweighs a Slack thread. Retrieval has no idea.

05

Answers live across sources.

"Migration at risk because A, B, and C" exists nowhere until something synthesizes it.

Giving an agent access and expecting it to understand is like hiring a new employee every morning and asking for decisions by lunch.

The shift

Retrieval finds fragments. XTrace maintains a worldview.

Most tools search at runtime — a scavenger hunt that starts from zero every time. XTrace maintains a continuously updated model of what your company knows. The answer doesn't get searched for. It already exists.

This is why we call it a belief revision engine, not a memory. Facts supersede, retract, and expire with full lineage. You can ask what did we used to believe? and the answer is real.

Common questions

FAQ

How is this different from Notion AI or ChatGPT memory? +

Those remember facts about you inside their walls. XTrace captures what your whole team produces across every tool, reasons about what's still true, and serves it anywhere.

How is this different from a vector DB or RAG pipeline? +

RAG searches at runtime from zero every time. XTrace maintains a persistent, conflict-resolved model of your company. The underlying tooling uses vectors; the product is fundamentally different.

How is this different from other "company brain" products? +

We ship a product, they ship a file format. XTrace has a UI, Context Agents, audience-aware redaction, and an E2E-encrypted Personal Space — plus XMem for developers who just want the plumbing.

Do I have to rip out my stack? +

No. XTrace reads from Notion, Slack, ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Granola, Drive, GitHub. Nothing moves.

Who can see what? +

Audience tiers scope views at the base and fact level. Personal Space is E2E encrypted — invisible to admins.

Is my data used to train models? +

Never. SOC 2 Type II, encryption at rest and in transit, VPC for Enterprise.

What happens to my data if I leave? +

You own it. Export as structured JSON any time.

How does Memory Hub relate to XMem? +

Memory Hub is the product your team uses. XMem is the same engine as a developer API. Same brain, two surfaces.
